Marine Ecosystem — Salish Sea Case Study
—
  • Reduced exposure of marine fauna to URN, per voyage / corridor
  • Reduced acoustic masking of Southern Resident killer whales (SRKW) and humpback whales
  • Reduced stress on salmon, other economically valuable fish, and forage fish
  • Increased acoustic detectability of vessels by humpback whales
  • Reduced biofouling (1)(3)(4)(6)
  • Improved habitat use
  • Improved SRKW and humpback whale prey detection and foraging success
  • Increased fish weight and improved survival probability
  • Increased avoidance response to vessels
  • Improved reproductive capacity and recovery potential of SRKW, humpback whales, salmon, and other economically valuable fish — in combination with prey-base and habitat measures
  • Reduced strike lethality at lower vessel speeds
  • Improved population status and diversity of sensitive marine species in shipping lanes
  • Increased phytoplankton biomass
  • Healthier and more resilient marine ecosystems in major shipping routes, supporting biodiversity conservation and the long-term provision of ocean ecosystem services
